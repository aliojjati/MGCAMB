MGCAMB

Modification of Growth with CAMB

Description

MGCAMB is a modified version of CAMB in which the linearized Einstein equations of General Relativity (GR) are modified.

Working with the perturbed FRW metric in the Newtonian gauge,


the modifications to the linear order Einstein equations are introduced in Fourier space in terms of two general functions of the wavenumber and time:

The parametrization is discussed in detail in this paper. The initial conditions for the evolution of linear perturbations are not modified in the current version of MGCAMB and a discussion of initial conditions can be found in the same paper. Some of the widely used functional forms for the two functions are implemented in the code, while other forms can also be easily implemented. Several examples can be found here.

Download

The versions of the MGCAMB patch appropriate for the different releases of CAMB can be obtained from here. The latest version (compatible with CAMB November 2015) can be obtained directly from here. Make sure to match the MGCAMB version with the CAMB version that you have to avoid compatibility issues.

Installation

Once you have downloaded the patch, you need to extract the compressed folder, replace the CAMB files with those just extracted and re-compile the code.

Another approach is to manually modify the source files following these instructions and then recompile the code.

Papers

The use of our patches is free, however, we request that you cite the following two papers:

  • Testing Gravity with CAMB and CosmoMC, A. Hojjati, L. Pogosian and G.B. Zhao, arXiv:1106.4543.
  • Searching for modified growth patterns with tomographic surveys, G.B. Zhao, L.Pogosian, A. Silvestri and J. Zylberberg, arXiv:0809.3791

Version History

February, 2016: Major upgrade (thanks to Alex Zucca) to CAMB November 2015 version, and CosmoMC July 2015 version. Planck data and PR2 likelihood are included.
Minor bug fixes in ISW terms.
MG functions are now defined in the "mgvariables" module at the beginning of equations.f90.
Added new models based on the m(a), β(a) parametrization discussed in this paper:

  • Hu-Sawicki model of f(R) gravity.
  • Generalized dilaton as in this paper.
  • Simpler dilaton model with β(a) = β0 a3.
  • Symmetron model as in this paper.

February, 2014: Major upgrade to CosmoMC, March 2013 version. Planck data can now be used.

December, 2013: Bug fixes thanks to Bin Hu and Jasson Dossett.

February, 2012: MGCAMB, January-12 version.

January, 2012: MGCAMB, October-11 version.

July, 2011: MGCAMB, July-11 version.

This version (V.2) of MGCAMB is written based on CAMB (instead of CAMBsources) which makes it easier to use with Cosmomc. It also generalizes the previous parametrization making it applicable to the entire range of redshifts and includes different components of the energy-momentum tensor. It also includes other modified-gravity parametrizations introduced in the literature.

The previous version of MGCAMB written by G.B. Zhao, L. Pogosian, A. Silvestri and J. Zylberberg was based on CAMBsources. That version modified GR equations for z>30, i.e. at late times when the contribution of radiation is negligible.
